Thank you for watching Tex and Xander! Any information you'll need should be below:

What is their typical schedule and do I need to stick to it? ▼

First of all, NO! You don't have to stick to it to a T. However, this is, of course, what they're used to:

Breakfast: some time between 8:30am and 10:00am

First walk: usually at around 9:00am, but at least before 12:00pm

Put away: they can be left alone for roughly 6 or 7 hours. Please no longer than that.

Second Walk: usually between 3:00pm and 6:00pm

Dinner: 6:00pm on the dot, Xander will make sure of it

Last Walk: usually around 11:00pm

What are the feeding instructions? ▼

Note: They can eat near each other, but please make sure to feed Xander first, and then Tex.

Breakfast: 1/2 Cup (measurement) of food for each dog, tell them to sit before giving them each their bowl.

Dinner: 1/2 Cup of dry food for Xander and 1/2 cup of food with a bit of water in it for Tex.

** If you're leaving right after giving them their food, they need dinner AND dessert. This is mostly because Xander becomes destructive if not. Prepare 2 kongs with either PB or Coconut oil. Give Xander his food, then tell Tex to go to his crate and give him his food. Then, grab the dessert and set it in Tex's crate and close him up. Last, give Xander his dessert on his bed *right* before leaving.

What do I do when I'm leaving? ▼

Prepare the kongs with a bit of Skippy peanut butter or coconut oil. You can also do a mixture of both. Add roughly half of a butter knife's worth inside of the kong. For Xander, make sure it's not too deep in the kong.

Tell Xander to go to his bed and sit first, and give it to him.

Tell Tex to go to his bed and sit, then give it to him. Tex's crate MUST be double latched.

Make sure the office door is shut, the master bathroom door is shut, and that all bags are up off of the ground. We keep ours at the kitchen table.

You may still come home to a mess. Sometimes Xander chews up our books.

Xander tore something up :/ ▼

-_- If it's ours, it's fine. Tell him to go to his bed and sit. Hold the item in his face and tell him bad boy a few times. Then ignore him for a while as punishment.

Please let us know if this happens.

It's time to walk them ▼

We switched the harnesses. Tex uses the black and grey harness. The black strap goes around his belly, the grey strap goes in front of his chest. Xander's is the blue harness and goes over his head and buckles on either side.

Dogs they hate: the people diagonal from us, Ms. Riece the Second's dog, and the guy who sometimes watches his Sister, whose a dog.

Xander must always poop in the mornings, but Tex doesn't have to. Sometimes they both poop twice a day and sometimes they only poop once. Just make sure Xander poops in the morning or he'll probably have an accident.

Commands: "on your bed", "let's go", "turn around", "let's go home", "do you want dinner?"

Afternoons ONLY: Tex needs to wear his shoes in the afternoon. When putting them on, be careful of his duclaw. He's not a fan of them, just fair warning. If you don't have time, it's raining, or any other reason you can't put them on, it's okay. Just use the baby whipes above the washing machine to clean off his paw after. The goal is to get the pollen off.

Tex is really itchy ▼

Call us or text us and we'll tell you which medicine to get out. You don't HAVE to give him medicine, unless he won't stop scratching and licking.

Is the storage closet locked? ▼

No, it's just really hard to open. We'll set out an HEB bag you can use inside for trash, so the only thing you need from the closet should be their food. Just keep twisting the knob and it'll eventually open.

What about the plants? ▼

They don't need watered. If a storm comes through or its very windy, you might have to bring them in. It's okay if you put them in the wrong places, just as long as they're inside. Any pots with dirt but NO plant in it can stay outside.

Where do the boys sleep? ▼

Since we aren't home, they can both sleep on the bed together. They should be fine, but if you want to be sure, make sure they don't fall asleep too close to each other.
